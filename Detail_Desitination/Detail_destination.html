Afar

Discover the breathtaking Awash Gorge, where 30-million-year-old geological formations reveal the Earth's ancient history. Carved by the Awash River, this dramatic landscape offers stunning cliffs, rugged terrain, and a glimpse into Ethiopia's prehistoric past. A haven for nature lovers and adventurers, the gorge is also home to diverse wildlife and offers incredible opportunities for hiking and photography.

Oromia Region

Bale Mountains National Park

Oromia

Explore Africa's largest alpine ecosystem, home to the endangered Ethiopian wolf and majestic mountain nyala. Trek across the otherworldly Sanetti Plateau - the largest Afro-alpine habitat on the continent - with its giant lobelia plants and breathtaking vistas.

Sof Omar Caves

Bale Zone

Venture into Africa's longest cave system, a 15km network of limestone chambers carved by the Weyb River. This sacred Islamic site features cathedral-like passages with 20m-high pillars, including the Chamber of Columns housing 300 natural pillars.

Wenchi Crater Lake

Oromia

Hike through cloud forests to this stunning volcanic crater lake surrounded by waterfalls and hot springs. Boat across crystal-clear waters to a 13th-century monastery on Tulu Gudo Island, passing grazing horses on lakeshore meadows.

Harenna Forest

Bale Mountains

Wander through Ethiopia's largest cloud forest, a misty realm of 40m-tall trees draped in moss and lichens. This biodiversity hotspot shelters wild coffee varieties, giant lobelias, and rare species like the Bale monkey.

Lake Ziway

Oromia

Discover a birdwatcher's paradise in this shallow Rift Valley lake hosting 300+ species including pink-backed pelicans and African fish eagles. Take a boat to Tulu Gudo Island's 12th-century monastery while spotting hippos in the marshes.

Southern Nations Region

Omo Valley Cultures

Southern Nations

Experience living anthropology with the Mursi, Hamar, and Karo peoples who maintain ancient traditions in this UNESCO World Heritage landscape. Witness elaborate body painting, bull-jumping ceremonies, and distinctive lip plates.

Tiya Megaliths

Southern Nations

Decipher Ethiopia's ancient history at this UNESCO site featuring 45 mysterious stelae dating to the 12th-14th centuries. The carved stones - some standing 5m tall - mark ancient burial sites with symbols of swords and plant-like motifs.

Konso Cultural Landscape

Southern Nations

Explore a UNESCO-protected terraced civilization where stone-walled villages have stood for 600 years. The Konso people's ingenious dry-stone terracing and irrigation systems sustain agriculture on steep slopes.

Dorze Traditions

Southern Nations

Visit the bamboo masters famous for their towering elephant-shaped houses woven from endemic highland bamboo. Watch traditional cotton weaving and taste kocho bread made from fermented false banana.

Bonga Rainforest

Kaffa Zone

Walk through the birthplace of Arabica coffee in Ethiopia's last remaining mid-altitude rainforest. Participate in traditional coffee ceremonies at source, learning how the Kaffa people first discovered coffee's energizing properties.

Gambella Region

Gambella National Park

Gambella

Witness Africa's second-largest mammal migration as over 1 million white-eared kob antelope traverse the floodplains. This vast wilderness shelters elephants, Nile lechwe, and chimpanzees in its mix of savannah and wetlands.
